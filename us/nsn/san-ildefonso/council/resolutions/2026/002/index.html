Resolution No. SI-R26-002: Impersonation of Pueblo Officials and Employees Law

At a duly called meeting of the Council of San Ildefonso Pueblo on the 3rd day of March, 2026, the following resolution was adopted:

WHEREAS, there is a need to protect the integrity of the Pueblo's duly constituted government, elected leadership, and employees, and prevent and deter false claims of authority to act on behalf of the Pueblo; and,

WHEREAS, the Council desires to protect the Pueblo's government-to-government relationships with the United States and other governmental entities and prevent fraud, confusion, interference with governmental functions, and reputational harm; and

WHEREAS, the Council desires to provide clear civil and criminal remedies for impersonation of Pueblo officials or employees.

NOW THEREFORE BE IT RESOLVED that the Council hereby enacts the attached resolution entitled the "THE IMPERSONATION OF PUEBLO OFFICIALS AND EMPLOYEES ACT".

CERTIFICATION

The foregoing resolution was duly voted upon by the Council on March 3rd, 2026, at a meeting with a vote of 8 in favor, 0 opposed, 1 abstaining and 1 absent (Governor presiding and not voting) pursuant to the authority vested in the Council by the Agreement of the Pueblo de San Ildefonso.

Denise Moquino, Council Secretary

IMPERSONATION OF PUEBLO OFFICIALS AND EMPLOYEES LAW

New Section 8.1.4.010

Section 1.1. Short Title

This enactment shall be known as the "Impersonation of Pueblo Officials and Employees Law" ("Act").

New Section 8.1.4.020

Section 1.2. Purpose and Authority

A. Purpose. The purpose of this resolution is to:

(a) Protect the integrity of the Pueblo's duly constituted government, elected leadership, and employees;

(b) Prevent and deter false claims of authority to act on behalf of the Pueblo;

(c) Protect the Pueblo's government-to-government relationships with the United States and other governmental entities;

(d) Prevent fraud, confusion, interference with governmental functions, and reputational harm; and

(e) Provide clear civil and criminal remedies for impersonation of Pueblo officials or employees.

B. Authority. This Law is enacted pursuant to the inherent sovereign authority of the San Ildefonso Pueblo, as reserved under its governing Agreement and recognized by federal law, to govern its internal affairs, protect its political integrity, regulate conduct affecting the Pueblo, and enforce its laws through civil and criminal remedies.

New Section 8.1.4.030

Section 1.3. Definitions

The Council hereby finds and declares that:

(a) "Pueblo" means the San Ildefonso Pueblo, a federally recognized Indian tribe, including all Pueblo departments, enterprises, boards, commissions, and instrumentalities.

(b) "Elected Official" means any individual duly elected or appointed in accordance with the governing Agreement and laws of the Pueblo, including but not limited to the Governor, Lieutenant Governor, and members of the Pueblo Council.

(c) "Pueblo Employee" means any individual employed by, contracted with, or otherwise authorized to act on behalf of the Pueblo or any Pueblo department or enterprise.

(d) "Impersonate" or "Impersonation" means knowingly and falsely representing oneself, directly or indirectly, by words, actions, documents, electronic communications, or other means, as: a. An elected official of the Pueblo; b. A Pueblo employee or authorized agent; or c. A person otherwise authorized to act on behalf of, speak for, or bind the Pueblo.

(e) "Knowingly" means that a person is aware that the representation is false or unauthorized, or acts with reckless disregard as to its truth or falsity.

(f) "Aggrieved Party" means the Pueblo or any individual or entity that suffers harm, confusion, reliance, interference, or potential liability as a result of conduct prohibited by this Law.

New Section 8.1.4.040

Section 1.4. Prohibited Acts

It is unlawful for any person, without lawful authority, to:

(1) Impersonate an elected official or Pueblo employee;

(2) Claim to be the "true," "rightful," or otherwise legitimate leader or governor of the Pueblo when such claim is not recognized under Pueblo law;

(3) Represent or imply to any federal, state, local, tribal, or other governmental entity or official that the person has authority to act for, represent, or bind the Pueblo;

(4) Attempt to conduct or participate in meetings, consultations, negotiations, or communications with any governmental or non-governmental entity while falsely claiming authority to represent the Pueblo;

(5) Use Pueblo titles, seals, letterhead, business cards, insignia, or other indicia of governmental authority in connection with an impersonation; or

(6) Aid, abet, conspire with, or assist another person in committing any act prohibited by this Law.

New Section 8.1.4.050

Section 1.5. Civil Cause of Action and Remedies

A. Standing. The Pueblo, acting through its Governor, Pueblo Council, or Prosecutor, or other duly authorized official, and any aggrieved party, may bring a civil action for violations of this Law.

B. Jurisdiction. The Courts of the Pueblo shall have exclusive original jurisdiction over civil actions arising under this Law.

C. Civil Remedies. Upon a finding that a violation has occurred, the Court may grant one or more of the following remedies:

1. Declaratory relief establishing that the defendant lacks authority to act on behalf of the Pueblo;

2. Temporary, preliminary, and permanent injunctive relief prohibiting further impersonation or misrepresentation;

3. Civil penalties in amounts not to exceed one thousand dollars ($1,000) per violation, with each act and each day of continued violation constituting a separate offense;

4. Restitution for any losses, costs, or damages incurred by the Pueblo or an aggrieved party;

5. Disgorgement of any benefit, compensation, or thing of value obtained through the prohibited conduct;

6. Court costs and reasonable attorneys' fees awarded to the prevailing Pueblo or aggrieved party; and

7. Any other relief the Court deems just and proper to protect the Pueblo's governmental interests.

D. Federal Cease-and-Desist Notices. Upon issuance of an injunction or declaratory judgment under this Section, the Court may authorize the Pueblo to transmit court-approved cease-and-desist notices to federal and state agencies or officials, or other interested parties, identifying the defendant as lacking authority to act on behalf of the Pueblo.

New Section 8.1.4.060

Section 1.6. Criminal Offense – Impersonation of a Pueblo Official or Employee

A. Offense Defined. A person commits the offense of impersonation of a Pueblo official or employee if the person knowingly impersonates an elected official, Pueblo employee, or authorized agent of the Pueblo with intent to induce another person or governmental entity to submit to such pretended authority, rely upon the impersonation, or act in response to the false representation.

B. Classification and Penalties. This offense shall be classified and punished in a manner consistent with the Pueblo's Criminal Code and governing Agreement limits. Penalties may include:

1. A fine not to exceed five thousand dollars ($5,000) per violation;

2. Incarceration for a period not to exceed one year per violation;

3. Probation, community service, or other lawful sanctions; or

4. Any combination of the foregoing.

Each act of impersonation, each communication, and each day the violation continues constitutes a separate offense.

New Section 8.1.4.070

Section 1.7. Enforcement and Official Certifications

A. Criminal Enforcement. This Law may be enforced by Pueblo law enforcement officers and prosecuted by the Pueblo, the prosecutor, or other authorized official.

B. Official Certifications of Authority. The Governor or his designee is authorized to issue written certifications identifying the individuals who are duly elected or authorized to act on behalf of the Pueblo. Such certifications may be provided to federal, state, or other governmental entities and shall constitute prima facie evidence of lawful authority.

New Section 8.1.4.080

Section 1.8. Sovereign Immunity

Nothing in this Law shall be construed as a waiver of the sovereign immunity of the San Ildefonso Pueblo, its officers, employees, or agents. Any waiver of sovereign immunity must be express and in writing.

New Section 8.1.4.090

Section 1.9. Severability

If any provision of this Law is held invalid or unenforceable, such invalidity shall not affect the remaining provisions, which shall continue in full force and effect.

New Section 8.1.4.100

Section 2.0. Effective Date

This Law shall take effect upon adoption by the Council and approval as required by the Agreement and laws of the San Ildefonso Pueblo.
