Resolution No. SI-R26-006: Pueblo de San Ildefonso Groundwater Quality Act

At a duly called meeting of the Council of San Ildefonso Pueblo on the 10th day of June, 2026, the following resolution was adopted:

WHEREAS, The Pueblo de San Ildefonso ("Pueblo"), a federally-recognized American Indian Tribe, hereby enacts the Pueblo de San Ildefonso Groundwater Quality Act ("Act") and asserts its sovereign authority over all actions taken which affect the use of water within the Pueblo; and,

WHEREAS, The Pueblo is the owner of full equitable title to groundwaters of the Pueblo as defined in Section 11.2.28.010(c), and that title resides undiminished in the Pueblo; the United States holds the legal title to those waters solely as trustee for the Pueblo; and,

WHEREAS, The groundwaters of the Pueblo are defined as: (1) all groundwaters reserved at any time for any purpose to the Pueblo, and to Pueblo lands, including any groundwaters which, in the course of nature or as the result of artificial works or artificial enhancement or weather modification methods, flow into or otherwise enhance such waters; (2) all groundwaters held by the Pueblo through prior or existing use, appropriation, purchase, contract, gift, bequest, or other means; (3) groundwaters which are contained within hydrologic systems located within the lands of the Pueblo; and (4) all groundwaters located beneath the surface of the lands held in trust by the United States of America for the Pueblo.

NOW THEREFORE BE IT RESOLVED that the Council hereby enacts the attached resolution entitled the "PUEBLO DE SAN ILDEFONSO GROUNDWATER QUALITY ACT".

CERTIFICATION

PUEBLO DE SAN ILDEFONSO GROUNDWATER QUALITY ACT

New Section 11.2.29.010

Section 1.1. Short Title

This enactment shall be known as the "Pueblo de San Ildefonso Groundwater Quality Act" ("Act").

New Section 11.2.29.020

Section 1.2. Purpose

The purpose of this resolution is to:

1. Protect, maintain, and improve the quality of the Pueblo's groundwater;

2. Designate the uses for which the groundwater of the Pueblo shall be protected;

3. Prescribe water quality standards (narrative and numeric) to sustain the uses of the groundwater of the Pueblo;

4. Assure that degradation of existing groundwater quality does not occur; and restoration occurs as appropriate;

5. Promote the health, social welfare, and economic well-being of the Pueblo, its people, and all the residents of Pueblo lands.

New Section 11.2.29.030

Section 1.3. Findings

The Council hereby finds and declares that:

(a) The Pueblo de San Ildefonso ("Pueblo"), a federally-recognized American Indian Tribe, hereby enacts the Pueblo de San Ildefonso Groundwater Quality Act ("Act") and asserts its sovereign authority over all actions taken which affect the use of water within the Pueblo; and,

(b) The Pueblo is the owner of full equitable title to groundwaters of the Pueblo as defined in Section 11.2.28.010(c), and that title resides undiminished in the Pueblo; the United States holds the legal title to those waters solely as trustee for the Pueblo; and,

(c) The groundwaters of the Pueblo are defined as: (1) all groundwaters reserved at any time for any purpose to the Pueblo, and to Pueblo lands, including any groundwaters which, in the course of nature or as the result of artificial works or artificial enhancement or weather modification methods, flow into or otherwise enhance such waters; (2) all groundwaters held by the Pueblo through prior or existing use, appropriation, purchase, contract, gift, bequest, or other means; (3) groundwaters which are contained within hydrologic systems located within the lands of the Pueblo; and (4) all groundwaters located beneath the surface of the lands held in trust by the United States of America for the Pueblo.

New Section 11.2.29.040

Section 1.4. Definitions

The following terms shall have the following meanings when used in this Act:

(a) "Clean Water Act" or "CWA" means the federal Clean Water Act, 33 U.S.C. §§ 1251-1387, as amended.

(b) "Council" means the Pueblo de San Ildefonso Council.

(c) "Criteria" means the elements of the Pueblo's water quality standards, expressed as constituent concentrations, levels, or narrative statements, representing a quality of water that supports a particular use. When criteria are met, water quality will generally protect the use.

(d) "Department" or "DECP" means the Department of Environmental Protection and Cultural Preservation.

(e) "Director" means the Director of the Department of Environmental and Cultural Preservation.

(f) "Governor" means the Pueblo de San Ildefonso Governor.

(g) "Groundwater" means water that collects or flows beneath the earth's surface, filling the porous spaces in soil, sediment, and rocks. Groundwater originates from rain and from melting snow and ice and is the source of water for aquifers, springs, and wells. The upper surface of groundwater is the water table.

(h) "Groundwater recharge" means the use of a surface water as a source of recharge to groundwater.

(i) "Groundwater use" means any direct, indirect, active, passive, present, or future benefit, value, function, service, or enjoyment associated with groundwater, including existence value, bequest value, future option value, ecological value, and any other benefits derived from the continued availability, quality, or integrity of the groundwater resource.

(j) "Nonpoint source" means any dispersed land-based or water-based activity rather than a point source that contributes to water quality degradation, including but not limited to, atmospheric deposition; surface water runoff from agricultural, urban, forest, construction and mining lands; subsurface or underground sources; or discharges from boats or marine vessels not otherwise regulated under the National Pollutant Discharge Elimination System program.

(k) "Person" means any individual, partnership, company, corporation, firm, association, or society, any federal, state, or local government or any of its programs, agencies, or other subdivisions, or any Indian Tribe, including the Pueblo, or any Pueblo agencies, divisions, departments, programs, enterprises, companies, or other entities.

(1) "Point source" means any discernible, confined, or discrete conveyance, including, but not limited to, any pipe, ditch, channel, sewer, tunnel, conduit, well, discrete fissure, container, confined animal feeding operation, vessel, or other floating craft, from which pollutants are or may be discharged.

(m) "Pollutant" means dredged spoil, solid waste, incinerator residue, filter backwash, sewage, garbage, sewage sludge, munitions, chemical wastes, biological materials, radioactive materials (except those regulated under the Atomic Energy Act of 1954, as amended (42 U.S.C. 2011 et seq.)), heat, wrecked or discarded equipment, rock, sand, cellar dirt and industrial, municipal, and agricultural waste discharged into water.

(n) "Pollution" means the man-made or man-induced alteration of the chemical, physical, biological, and radiological integrity of water.

(o) "Pueblo" means the Pueblo de San Ildefonso.

(p) "Respond" or "response" means any action taken to address actual or threatened groundwater contamination. The term includes, but is not limited to, actions to investigate, assess, monitor, prevent, minimize, mitigate, contain, remove, treat, remediate, or otherwise address contamination or its effects on public health, welfare, or the environment.

(q) "Supreme Court" means the Pueblo de San Ildefonso Supreme Court.

(r) "Trial Court" means the Pueblo de San Ildefonso Trial Court.

New Section 11.2.29.050

Section 1.5. Department of Environmental and Cultural Preservation

Pursuant its authority under Part 23.1.1 of the Pueblo de San Ildefonso Code, the Department of Environmental and Cultural Preservation shall serve under the direction of the Governor as the lead Department in the implementation of this Act, and the Director shall be the lead Pueblo official in the implementation of this Act. The Department shall work in cooperation with the New Mexico Environment Department, U.S. Environmental Protection Agency and other federal, tribal, or state agencies when necessary to implement this Act.

New Section 11.2.29.060

Section 1.6. Severability

If any provision of this Act or the application thereof to any person or circumstance is held invalid, the remainder of these regulations and the application of such provision to other persons or circumstances shall remain unaffected, and to this end the provisions of these regulations are declared to be severable.

New Section 11.2.29.070

Section 1.7. Antidegradation Policy and Implementation Plan

(a) Antidegradation Policy

1. All groundwater uses and the level of groundwater quality necessary to support those uses shall be maintained and protected. The Director may use any and all available regulatory and enforcement means to seek full restoration of any groundwater requiring restoration. Groundwater quality shall not be degraded except as specifically authorized under this Act.

2. The level of water quality shall be maintained and protected unless it is found by the Governor and Council, after full satisfaction of governmental requirements, that a lower level of water quality is required to accommodate important economic or social development in the area. In no case shall water quality be allowed to be decreased below that necessary to fully maintain the groundwater uses. In permitting any degradation of water quality, the Pueblo shall impose appropriate requirements for all new and existing point sources and reasonable Best Management Practices (BMPs) for non-point source control.

(b) Implementation Plan

1. The DECP shall work with the Governor and Council to implement these standards, including the antidegradation policy, by working to establish and maintain appropriate controls on the introduction of pollutants into Pueblo groundwaters including by taking the actions listed below in coordination with federal, tribal, and state agencies, as appropriate:

2. Designate Pueblo groundwaters and determine numeric values for background levels for contaminants;

3. Conduct monitoring of Pueblo groundwater to assess the physical and chemical factors relative to heavy metals and toxic substance contamination;

4. Coordinate its groundwater protection activities with other tribal governments, local, state, and federal agencies as appropriate;

5. Conduct an antidegradation analysis for regulated actions that may potentially impair water quality and advocate for regulatory actions that achieve the Pueblo's antidegradation goals.

6. Develop and pursue inspection and enforcement programs to ensure that groundwater quality complies with the Act and satisfies the requirements of any related regulations which the Pueblo may enact after the adoption of this Act and accompanying standards;

7. Evaluate the effectiveness of BMPs selected to prevent or abate non-point sources of water pollutants;

8. Recommend to the Governor permitting or management practices, which would be consistent with the purposes of the Act.

New Section 11.2.29.080

New Section 11.2.29.090

Section 1.9. Prohibition on Degradation of Pueblo Groundwater

No person shall contaminate, threaten to contaminate, or trespass in any manner upon the Pueblo's groundwater. All groundwater uses shall be protected; that is, the level of water quality necessary to maintain those uses is maintained, and any such degradation is prohibited.

New Section 11.2.29.100

Section 2.0. Use of Pueblo Groundwater

(a) The Governor and the Director are hereby authorized to permit the use of Pueblo groundwater for purposes consistent with this Act and for the benefit of the Pueblo and its members, including the protection of public health, welfare, cultural values, and economic development.

(b) The Director shall develop a Groundwater Use Plan and submit such Plan to the Council for review and approval.

(c) The Governor and Director shall authorize uses of Pueblo groundwater only as consistent with the approved Groundwater Use Plan and this Act.

New Section 11.2.29.110

Section 2.1. Unlawful Acts

It shall be unlawful to contaminate, threaten to contaminate, or otherwise trespass upon Pueblo groundwater or cause a violation of the Act. Each day that a person violates the Act shall constitute a separate violation. Each violation with respect to (1) each separate point source or nonpoint source (2) each separate parameter or standard violated and (3) each separate discharge of contaminants to the groundwater shall constitute a separate violation.

New Section 11.2.29.120

Section 2.2. Public Nuisance

(a) Any actual or threatened contamination of the Pueblo's groundwater that causes or contributes to a violation of this Act, or that imminently threatens such violation, is hereby declared to be a public nuisance.

(b) A public nuisance under this Section shall include, but is not limited to:

1. Any activity that degrades or threatens to degrade the chemical, physical, or biological integrity of the Pueblo's groundwater;

2. Any activity that renders or is likely to render groundwater harmful, detrimental, or injurious to public health, safety, welfare, cultural uses, or ecological functions;

3. Any activity that interferes with the Pueblo's present or future use, protection, or restoration of groundwater resources;

4. Any activity occurring within or outside the Pueblo that causes or contributes, directly or indirectly, to groundwater contamination within the Pueblo lands or groundwater systems subject to Pueblo jurisdiction.

(c) The declaration of a public nuisance under this Section is independent of, and in addition to, any other prohibitions provided in this Act or under Pueblo laws.

(d) The Pueblo may abate any public nuisance declared under this Section through administrative orders, civil actions, injunctive relief, or any other lawful means.

New Section 11.2.29.130

Section 2.3. Civil Penalties

(a) Any person who violates any provision of the Act shall be subject to a civil penalty up to

$68,445 for each violation per day.

(b) In determining the amount of a civil penalty, the DECP or the Trial Court shall consider the seriousness of the violation or violations, the economic benefit to the violator, if any, resulting from the violation, any history of such violations, any good-faith efforts to comply with applicable requirements, and such other factors as justice may require.

New Section 11.2.29.140

Section 2.4. Damages and Cost Recovery

(a) Any person who violates any provision of the Act shall be liable to the Pueblo for all damages to Pueblo natural resources and groundwater uses caused by such violation, as well as all reasonable costs incurred by the Pueblo to respond to the conditions caused by the violation, including enforcement costs, incurred consistent with this Act.

(b) Damages caused by a violation shall be determined by the DECP or the Trial Court based upon:

1. the higher of the economic benefit to the violator or the detriment to the Pueblo (including, but not limited to, lost use and lost opportunity costs);

2. the costs of enforcement (including, but not limited to, costs associated with investigation of violations and reasonable attorney and expert witness fees and costs);

3. the cost of any response action taken or ordered by the Director, or their designee, to restore the groundwater to a point of compliance with the Pueblo's Groundwater Quality Standards;

4. the reasonable cost of a damage assessment and full restoration;

5. all other consequential or special damages proximately flowing from the violation, including, but not limited to, compensation for the interim loss of groundwater use pending recovery and replacement water if such violation of the Act contaminates or threatens to contaminate Pueblo water supplies.

New Section 11.2.29.150

Section 2.5. Information and Investigation

(a) Any person may provide information to the DECP in writing concerning facts which indicate the contamination, threatened contamination, or a trespass upon Pueblo groundwater.

(b) Upon receipt of information indicating the contamination, threatened contamination, or a trespass upon Pueblo groundwater may be occurring, the DECP shall:

1. Notify such Pueblo members whose water supply is or may be affected by the contamination, threatened contamination, or a trespass upon Pueblo groundwater; and

2. Undertake such investigation as may be necessary to determine whether and how to proceed to protect and restore the Pueblo groundwater.

(c) The DECP, or its designee, may notify any divisions, departments, and programs of the Pueblo Government, which, in the Director's discretion, need to be informed of contamination, threatened contamination or a trespass upon Pueblo groundwater.

(d) The DECP may require any person who has or may have information relevant to the investigation into the potential contamination, threat of contamination, or trespass upon Pueblo groundwater to furnish, upon reasonable notice, information or documents relating to such matters. In addition, upon reasonable notice, such person shall grant the DECP or their designee access at all reasonable times to the property at issue to inspect and copy all documents or records relating to such matters or copy and furnish such documents or records at the option and expense of such person.

(e) The DECP, or its designee, may enter any property at any time during normal business hours, and at other reasonable times, to inspect, investigate, or monitor activities which in the DECP's judgment may be contaminating or threatening to contaminate, or trespass upon Pueblo groundwater.

(f) The DECP, or its designee, has the authority to collect samples of soil, groundwater, or other materials and take any other appropriate investigatory actions, which in the Director's discretion are necessary to determine whether contamination, threat of contamination or trespass upon Pueblo groundwater has occurred.

(g) If, after investigation, the Director finds that contamination, threatened contamination, or a trespass upon Pueblo groundwater is occurring or has occurred and poses an immediate hazard to the public health or safety to the Pueblo or current or future groundwater uses, the Director may take immediate action necessary to prevent or stop the contamination or threatened contamination, protect the public uses, and mitigate any damage to Pueblo groundwater, including taking any steps to remove and/or confiscate any potential sources of contamination.

(h) The Director shall consult with Pueblo members whose water supply is or may be affected by the contamination, threatened contamination, or a trespass upon groundwater prior to determining how to proceed when the DECP has determined that Pueblo groundwater has been impacted.

(i) Any costs or fees incurred by the DECP or their designee in carrying out actions under this Section must be paid by the person who contaminated or threatened to contaminate or trespassed upon Pueblo groundwater as provided for in this Section.

(j) The Director shall have the authority to designate one or more employees of the DECP as designees for purposes of implementing this Act and taking authorized investigative actions pursuant to this Act. The Director or his designee may use properly qualified contractors under the direct Supervision of the Director or his designee to implement the provisions of this Act.

New Section 11.2.29.160

Section 2.6. Enforcement

(a) Whenever the Director finds that any person has caused a violation of any provisions of the Act, the Director may take any of the following actions or combination of actions:

1. Issue a Notice of Trespass to Pueblo Groundwater and Order to Comply in accordance with Section 11.2.29.170 of this Act;

2. Issue a Notice of Assessment of Penalty, Damages and Costs in accordance with Section 11.2.29.180 of this Act;

3. Request that the Pueblo bring a civil action in accordance with Section 11.2.29.250 of this Act.

(b) The Director shall have the authority to designate one or more employees of the DECP as a designee(s) for purposes of taking authorized enforcement actions pursuant to this Act.

(c) Any decisions by the Director or his designee in the enforcement of this Act shall be reviewed by any reviewing Court, under the arbitrary and capricious or otherwise contrary to applicable law standard as developed under the Federal Administrative Procedures Act based on the administrative record before the Director at the time the decision was made.

New Section 11.2.29.170

Section 2.7. Notice of Trespass to Pueblo Groundwater and Order to Comply

(a) A Notice of Trespass to Pueblo Groundwater and Order to Comply shall be in writing and shall identify the person who is contaminating, threatening to contaminate, or trespassing upon Pueblo Groundwater, the location and date of the contamination, threatened contamination or trespass upon Pueblo Groundwater, and shall contain a short statement of facts indicating the nature and circumstances of the contamination, threatened contamination, or trespass upon Pueblo Groundwater.

(b) The Notice and Order shall specify what action shall be taken by the person who has contaminated, threatened to contaminate, or trespassed upon Pueblo Groundwater. Action required may include:

1. Halting of discharges from point sources and/or nonpoint sources;

2. Undertaking an investigation;

3. Requiring access to property for sampling and other investigation;

4. Paying all related attorneys and expert fees and costs;

5. Remediating any groundwater degradation and bringing affected Pueblo Groundwater back into compliance with this Act;

6. Complying with the terms, conditions, covenants or restrictions of any applicable laws, regulations, or permit; and

7. Such other action as may be necessary to cure a violation.

(c) The Notice and Order shall specify a reasonable period of time within which to comply.

(d) The Notice and Order shall advise the person identified therein of the rights available under Parts 11.2.29.220, 11.2.29.230, and 11.2.29.240 of this Act.

(e) The Notice and Order shall be served upon the person identified in the notice in the same manner as a summons and complaint pursuant to the Pueblo Rules of Civil Procedure, or as otherwise specified in an applicable agreement or permit.

(f) Nothing in this Section shall prohibit the assessment of penalties and/or damages for contamination, threatened contamination, or trespass upon Pueblo Groundwater occurring prior to service of a Notice and Order or prior to the completion of any compliance period provided for by a Notice or Order.

New Section 11.2.29.180

Section 2.8. Notice of Assessment of Penalty, Damages and Costs

(a) A Notice of Assessment of Penalty, Damages and Costs may be served with a Notice of Trespass to Pueblo Groundwater and Order to Comply or at any time thereafter.

(b) The Notice of Assessment of Penalty, Damages and Costs shall be in writing and shall identify the person who contaminated, threatened contamination, or trespassed upon Pueblo Groundwater, the location and date of the contamination, threatened contamination, or trespass upon Pueblo Groundwater, and shall contain a short statement of facts indicating the nature and circumstances of the contamination, threatened contamination, or trespass upon Pueblo Groundwater.

(c) The Notice shall specify:

1. the amount of penalties assessed in accordance with Section 11.2.29.130 of this Act;

2. the amount of damages and costs assessed in accordance with Section 11.2.29.140 of this Act;

3. the amount of attorneys' and experts' fees and costs; and

4. the time period for which assessment is made.

(d) Each item of assessment of penalty, damages, fees, and/or costs shall reference the provisions of this Act, which govern the assessment, and shall be accompanied by a short description of the method the Director used to determine the amount of said assessment.

(e) The Notice shall specify the date payment is due to the Pueblo and shall recite the provisions of Sections 11.2.29.190(a), (b), and (c) of this Act.

(f) The Notice shall advise the person identified therein of the rights available under Sections 11.2.29.230, 11.2.29.240, and 11.2.29.250 of this Act.

(g) The Notice shall be served upon the person identified in the Notice in the same manner as a summons and complaint pursuant to the Pueblo Rules of Civil Procedure, or as otherwise specified in an applicable permit or agreement.

New Section 11.2.29.190

Section 2.9. Payment of Penalties, Damages, Fees, and Costs

(a) Penalties, damages, costs, and fees assessed pursuant to Sections 11.2.29.130 and 11.2.29.140 of this Act shall be due and payable upon receipt of notice thereof and shall become delinquent thirty (30) calendar days thereafter.

(b) Payments shall be made by certified check payable to the order of the Pueblo de San Ildefonso.

(c) Interest on any delinquent payment of penalties, damages, costs, and fees shall accrue from the date such payment becomes delinquent pursuant to Section 11.2.29.190(a) of this Act. Said interest shall be assessed at the rate of two percent (2%) per month compounded until said assessment is paid or otherwise discharged.

(d) Monies collected through payment of penalties, damages, costs, and fees, together with any interest thereon, shall be deposited in the Pueblo's Groundwater Enforcement Fund.

(e) A Pueblo Groundwater Enforcement Fund is hereby established.

1. The Pueblo Groundwater Enforcement Fund will be comprised of proceeds received from penalties, damages, costs, and fees assessed pursuant to Sections 11.2.29.130 and 11.2.29.140 of this Act.

2. Expenditures may be allocated from the Pueblo Groundwater Enforcement Fund only for construction, repair, operation, or maintenance of measures to protect Pueblo Groundwater consistent with this Act.

3. Expenditures from the Pueblo Groundwater Enforcement Fund shall be administered and managed by the Director. Annual Expenditure reports shall be submitted to Council for their review.

New Section 11.2.29.200

Section 3.0. Notice to Council and Governor

The Director shall deliver a copy of the Notice of Trespass to Pueblo Groundwater and Order to Comply and the Notice of Assessment of Penalty, Damages and Costs to the Council and the Governor.

New Section 11.2.29.210

Section 3.1. Administrative Record

(a) The Director shall be responsible for compiling an Administrative Record for each Notice of Trespass to Pueblo Groundwater and Order to Comply and Notice of Assessment of Penalty, Damages and Costs.

(b) Such record shall consist of any and all information, data, evidence, or report that the Director relied upon when issuing the Notice of Trespass to Pueblo Groundwater and Order to Comply, or Notice of Assessment of Penalty, Damages and Costs.

(c) The Administrative Record shall include any Notices of Appeal and any documents submitted with such notice pursuant to Section 11.2.29.220 of this Act, including any reports supporting such appeal.

(d) Such record may be reviewed by any member of the public upon proper request. All reasonable costs associated with complying with such request shall be paid by the person making such request.

New Section 11.2.29.220

Section 3.2. Appeals

(a) Any person who receives a Notice of Trespass to Pueblo Groundwater and Order to Comply or Notice of Assessment of Penalty, Damages and Costs pursuant to Sections 11.2.29.170 or 11.2.29.180 of this Act may appeal by filing with the Director a Notice of Appeal within fifteen (15) calendar days of receipt of such Notice.

(b) The Notice of Appeal shall be in writing and shall:

1. identify the person to whom notice is addressed, the location and date at issue, and shall contain a short statement of facts indicating the nature and circumstances of the contamination, threatened contamination, or trespass upon Pueblo Groundwater;

2. specify the Notice or Order being appealed;

3. specify the legal or factual basis for the appeal; and

4. include documents, reports, or affidavits from experts or witnesses in support of the party's position on appeal.

(c) Upon the receipt of a Notice of Appeal, the Director shall refer the appeal to the Council, who shall, within ten (10) calendar days, assign the appeal to a qualified and impartial hearing officer and notify the parties of the name of that hearing officer and the telephone number and address where he or she can be reached.

New Section 11.2.29.230

Section 3.3. Stay Upon Appeal

(a) The filing of a Notice of Appeal with the Director shall not stay the enforcement of the Notice of Trespass to Pueblo. However, an appellant may request the hearing officer to grant a stay of enforcement any time after filing the Notice of Appeal but before the issuance of a decision by the hearing officer. A stay may be granted by the hearing officer where justice so requires, and upon such terms and conditions as the hearing officer may

find just and proper. An order granting a stay shall be in writing and shall state the grounds therefor and the terms and conditions thereof.

(b) A stay shall not be granted if the contamination, threatened contamination, or trespass upon Pueblo Groundwater poses a substantial and imminent threat to a drinking water supply source.

(c) An order granting or denying a stay, and any terms and conditions thereof, shall be final and may be appealed to the Trial Court or other court of competent jurisdiction within fifteen (15) calendar days of its issuance, pursuant to Section 11.2.29.250 of this Act.

New Section 11.2.29.240

Section 3.4. Hearing and Decision

(a) The Hearing Officer shall hear the appeal within thirty (30) calendar days of the date the Hearing Officer was assigned to the appeal by the Council pursuant to Section 11.2.29.220(c). Upon request of a party, reasonable continuances may be granted.

(b) Notice shall be afforded to the parties at least ten (10) calendar days in advance of the date set for hearing. Each party at the hearing may be represented by counsel. Except with respect to a challenge to a response action, each party shall have the opportunity to provide written submissions, subpoena witnesses, present evidence, and examine witnesses. Notices of subpoenas, written submissions, lists of witnesses and exhibits must be received by the Hearing Officer and all other parties to the hearing at least five (5) calendar days in advance of the hearing or as otherwise required by the Hearing Officer.

(c) Review of any issues concerning the adequacy of any response action taken or ordered by the Director, or his designee, including, but not limited to, the actions taken or ordered by the Director pursuant to Sections 11.2.29.140(b)(3), 11.2.29.150(b)(2), 11.2.29.150(e), 11 .2.29.150(f), 11.2.29.150(g) of this Act shall be limited to the Administrative Record. The Hearing Officer shall uphold the Director's, or his designee's, decision with regards to any response action unless the objecting party can demonstrate, on the Administrative Record, that the decision was arbitrary and capricious or otherwise not in accordance with law.

(d) Within twenty (20) calendar days after the hearing, the Hearing Officer shall issue a written decision stating the grounds therefor. The Hearing Officer may uphold, reverse, or modify the decision of the Director or any part thereof, but may not grant any other relief.

(e) The decisions of the Hearing Officer shall be final and may be appealed by any party to the Trial Court or other court of competent jurisdiction pursuant to Section 11.2.29.250 of this Act.

New Section 11.2.29.250

Section 3.5. Judicial Review and Enforcement

(a) The written decision of the Hearing Officer made pursuant to Sections 11.2.29.230 and 11.2.29.240 of this Act may be appealed by any party within thirty (30) calendar days of its issuance to the Trial Court or other court of competent jurisdiction.

(b) All appeals to the Trial Court or other court of competent jurisdiction shall be made in accordance with the Pueblo Rules of Civil Procedure, or other applicable rules.

(c) Judicial review of any issues concerning the adequacy of any response action taken or ordered by the Director, or his designee, including, but not limited to, the actions taken or ordered by the Director pursuant to Sections 11.2.29.140(b)(3), 11.2.29.150(b)(2), 11.2.29.150(e), 11.2.29.150(f), 11.2.29.150(g) of this Act, shall be limited to the Administrative Record. The court shall uphold the Director's, or his designees, decision with regards to any response action unless the objecting party can demonstrate, on the Administrative Record, that the decision was arbitrary and capricious or otherwise not in accordance with law.

(d) At the request of the Director or otherwise, the Pueblo may bring a civil action in any court of competent jurisdiction to enjoin any contamination, threatened contamination or trespass upon Pueblo Groundwater or to obtain payment or enforcement of any assessment of damages, penalties, costs and/or fees, or for such other relief as may be authorized or allowed under any applicable law.
