24.2.12.030 Findings

The Council hereby finds and declares that:

(a) Comcast Cable Communications Management, LLC ("Comcast") owns a fiber optic line which crosses over the Pueblo's lands.

(b) The Bureau of Indian Affairs ("BIA") executed a lease agreement on March 12, 1980 with Los Alamos Cable TV which authorized television cable to be buried along State Road 4 on the Pueblo's lands.

(c) The initial lease term with Los Alamos Cable TV began on March 17, 1980 and was for a period of twenty (20) years with an annual payment amount of $3,000, with an option for an additional twenty (20) year term with rent to be renegotiated at the end of that first twenty (20) year term. The Lease also contained a provision for a rent adjustment at five (5) year intervals and a provision for interest on late payments.

(d) Los Alamos Cable TV was subsequently acquired by Comcast, although the date of acquisition by Comcast is unknown, and Comcast assumed the Los Alamos Cable TV lease apparently without notice to the Pueblo or the BIA.

(e) Comcast at some point since 1980 has replaced the television cable with a fiber optic line without notice to or approval by the Pueblo or the BIA.

(f) It appears that Comcast exercised the option to renew for a second twenty (20) year term but it is unknown if Comcast notified the Pueblo or the BIA of their intention to do so and the rental payment adjustment did not occur at any point over the lease term.

(g) The lease expired on March 17, 2020.

(h) The Pueblo and Comcast have negotiated terms to reconcile compensation owed to the Pueblo or paid by Comcast under the prior lease and terms for a new Right-of-Way Agreement for the fiber optic line.

(i) The Pueblo and Comcast have negotiated terms for a Right-of-Way for a twenty-year (20) term to begin on March 17, 2020 and end on March 16, 2040.

(j) The Pueblo and Comcast have agreed upon total compensation in the amount of $1,168,810.61 which includes compensation for past use under the prior lease and compensation for the new Right-of-Way.

(k) The Council has been fully briefed and has reviewed the proposed Right-of-Way Agreement terms and has determined that it is in the best interest of the Pueblo to grant a limited waiver of the Pueblo's immunity from suit to enforce the Right-of-Way Agreement and authorize the Governor to sign a form of the Right-of-Way Agreement.

(l) The Council also finds it is in the best interest of the Pueblo to authorize the BIA to grant an Easement for Right-of-Way to Comcast in the same or similar form as attached hereto as Exhibit A, and to waive the requirement for a bond as Comcast has made a substantial partial payment toward the total compensation due, and to waive the valuation requirement and accept the agreed upon compensation negotiated between the Pueblo and Comcast.

(November 18, 2025, SI-R25-010.)
