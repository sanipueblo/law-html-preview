11.2.29.150 Information and Investigation

(a) Any person may provide information to the DECP in writing concerning facts which indicate the contamination, threatened contamination, or a trespass upon Pueblo groundwater.

(b) Upon receipt of information indicating the contamination, threatened contamination, or a trespass upon Pueblo groundwater may be occurring, the DECP shall:

(1) Notify such Pueblo members whose water supply is or may be affected by the contamination, threatened contamination, or a trespass upon Pueblo groundwater; and

(2) Undertake such investigation as may be necessary to determine whether and how to proceed to protect and restore the Pueblo groundwater.

(c) The DECP, or its designee, may notify any divisions, departments, and programs of the Pueblo Government, which, in the Director's discretion, need to be informed of contamination, threatened contamination or a trespass upon Pueblo groundwater.

(d) The DECP may require any person who has or may have information relevant to the investigation into the potential contamination, threat of contamination, or trespass upon Pueblo groundwater to furnish, upon reasonable notice, information or documents relating to such matters. In addition, upon reasonable notice, such person shall grant the DECP or their designee access at all reasonable times to the property at issue to inspect and copy all documents or records relating to such matters or copy and furnish such documents or records at the option and expense of such person.

(e) The DECP, or its designee, may enter any property at any time during normal business hours, and at other reasonable times, to inspect, investigate, or monitor activities which in the DECP's judgment may be contaminating or threatening to contaminate, or trespass upon Pueblo groundwater.

(f) The DECP, or its designee, has the authority to collect samples of soil, groundwater, or other materials and take any other appropriate investigatory actions, which in the Director's discretion are necessary to determine whether contamination, threat of contamination or trespass upon Pueblo groundwater has occurred.

(g) If, after investigation, the Director finds that contamination, threatened contamination, or a trespass upon Pueblo groundwater is occurring or has occurred and poses an immediate hazard to the public health or safety to the Pueblo or current or future groundwater uses, the Director may take immediate action necessary to prevent or stop the contamination or threatened contamination, protect the public uses, and mitigate any damage to Pueblo groundwater, including taking any steps to remove and/or confiscate any potential sources of contamination.

(h) The Director shall consult with Pueblo members whose water supply is or may be affected by the contamination, threatened contamination, or a trespass upon groundwater prior to determining how to proceed when the DECP has determined that Pueblo groundwater has been impacted.

(i) Any costs or fees incurred by the DECP or their designee in carrying out actions under this Section must be paid by the person who contaminated or threatened to contaminate or trespassed upon Pueblo groundwater as provided for in this Section.

(j) The Director shall have the authority to designate one or more employees of the DECP as designees for purposes of implementing this Act and taking authorized investigative actions pursuant to this Act. The Director or his designee may use properly qualified contractors under the direct Supervision of the Director or his designee to implement the provisions of this Act.

(June 12, 2026, SI-R26-006.)
