4.4.1.070 Duties of the Courts

(a) Create a Budget: The Supreme Court will propose an Annual Budget for approval by the Council, in accordance with the AGREEMENT and the Council's budget process.

(b) Hire Clerks and other necessary Court Personnel: The Trial Court and Supreme Court will hire and supervise their respective Court staff, consistent with the Pueblo's Personnel Policy Manual. In the absence of either Clerk, the other Clerk may perform duties for both Courts.

(c) Prepare Reports: The Trial Judge and Chief Justice shall, after soliciting comments and input from their Clerks, prepare semi-annual reports for their respective Courts, describing the functioning of their respective courts, including budgetary performance and projections, and setting forth specific recommendations for improvements. The Supreme Court will submit the combined reports to the Council.

(d) Administer the Courts: The Chief Judge of the Trial Court Chief Justice of the Supreme Court shall be responsible for the administration of their respective Courts, shall assign cases, and manage their Court' calendar and business. The Chief Trial Judge may designate an Associate Judge to act as Chief Judge in his absence. The Chief Justice of the Supreme Court may designate an Associate Justice to act as Chief Judge in his absence.

(e) Appointment of Temporary Judges: In the absence of a Chief Judge, the Chief Justice of the Supreme Court, in collaboration with other Justices, shall appoint a temporary judge or judges until a Chief Judge is selected by the Council.

(May 10, 2012, SI-R12-016.)
