Title 14 SOCIAL SERVICES

Chapter 14.1 Health and Human Services

Part 14.1.1 Department of Health and Human Services Enabling Act

Editor's Notes

This law was originally enacted pursuant to Council Resolution No. SI-R12-022, dated June 18, 2012.

14.1.1.010 Findings

(a) Annual budget items may only be included if they have first been authorized by law as required by Section 5(a)(ii)(A) of the Agreement;

(b) The Council finds that reorganizing the structure of governmental departments and programs will promote efficiency within the Tribe;

(c) The Council finds that establishing a Department of Health and Human Services will assist the Tribe in assessing and implementing various health care programs in a well-coordinated and collaborative manner that will enhance educational awareness, disease prevention, and healthy behavioral change.

(June 18, 2012, SI-R12-022.)

14.1.1.020 Short Title

This enactment shall be known as the Department of Health and Human Services Enabling Act ("Act").

(June 18, 2012, SI-R12-022.)

14.1.1.030 Definitions

(a) "Agreement" means the Pueblo de San Ildefonso governing Agreement.

(b) "Council" means the Pueblo de San Ildefonso Council.

(c) "Pueblo" means the Pueblo de San Ildefonso.

(June 18, 2012, SI-R12-022.)

14.1.1.040 Purpose

The Purpose of the Act is to establish the Department of Health and Human Services.

(June 18, 2012, SI-R12-022.)

14.1.1.050 Findings

The Council hereby finds and declares that:

(a) reorganizing the structure of governmental departments and programs will promote efficiency within the Tribe; and

(b) establishing a Department of Health and Services will assist the Tribe in assessing and implementing various health care programs.

(June 18, 2012, SI-R12-022.)

14.1.1.060 General Policy

(a) Department of Health and Human Services. This act shall establish the Department of Health and Human Services to in assess and implement various health care programs in a well-coordinated and collaborative manner that will enhance educational awareness, disease prevention, and healthy behavioral change.

(June 18, 2012, SI-R12-022.)

14.1.1.070 Organization of the Department of Health and Human Services

(a) There shall be a Director of the Department of Health and Human Services who shall oversee the general supervision of the department.

(b) In order to promote efficiency within the Department of Health and Human Services, program offices will be established as necessary. A program director shall oversee the general supervision of the each program office and shall report to the Director of the Department of Health and Human Services.

(c) The Department of Health and Human Services includes the following program offices:

(1) Diabetes Prevention Program

(2) Healthy Kids

(3) Community Health Representatives ("CHR")

(4) Indian Child Welfare Act Program ("ICWA")

(June 18, 2012, SI-R12-022.)

14.1.1.080 Department of Health and Human Services Goals and Objectives

Health & Human Services

The mission of the Department of Health & Human Services ("Department") is to assess and implement various health care programs (Diabetes Program, Community Health Representatives, Social Services, and Healthy Kids Initiative) in a well-coordinated and collaborative manner that will enhance educational awareness, disease prevention, and healthy behavioral changes. The primary goal of the Department is to provide comprehensive health care awareness that will target multiple dimensions of health such as physical, mental, emotional, and social aspects of Pueblo members of all ages.

Diabetes Prevention Program

The Diabetes Prevention program office promotes prevention education and diabetes self-management education, maintains data on the identified diabetic population within the community measures diabetes care outcomes, and works with outside entities to meet community health needs and improves services.

Healthy Kids

The Healthy Kids program office promotes community health by implementing alcohol, drug, and tobacco prevention programs focusing on youth. The Healthy Kids program office shall lead the community's health council initiatives.

Community Health Representative ("CHR") Program

The CHR program office provides management to improve and maintain the delivery systems for general health care, gerontological health care, maternal and child health, and environmental health. The CHR program office shall coordinate with health resource agencies to assure the provision of necessary health care services to Pueblo members.

Indian Child Welfare Act Program ("ICWA")

The ICWA program office manages the Pueblo's child welfare system including working toward protecting the children of the Pueblo from child abuse, neglect, and the separation of families through collaborative work with families. The ICWA program office shall provide a central contact point for families seeking assistance in temporary and permanent placement of children.

(June 18, 2012, SI-R12-022.)

14.1.1.090 Amendment

The Council may amend this enabling act or reorganize the Department of Health and Human Services at any time upon majority vote of the Council.

(June 18, 2012, SI-R12-022.)

14.1.1.100 Sovereign Immunity

Nothing in this Act waives the sovereign immunity of the Pueblo or any of its representatives, officers, or employees.

(June 18, 2012, SI-R12-022.)

14.1.1.110 Severability

If a court of competent jurisdiction finds any provision of this Act to be invalid or illegal under applicable tribal and/or federal law, such provision shall be severed from this Act and the remainder of this Act shall remain in full force and effect.

(June 18, 2012, SI-R12-022.)

Part 14.1.2 Approving the Pueblo de San Ildefonso's Fy2016 BIA Indian Highway Child Passenger Safety Seat Program

14.1.2.010 Findings

(a) The Bureau of Indian Affairs, Office of Justice Services, Northern Pueblo Agency is responsible for administering, developing, and implementing organizational objectives for Law Enforcement, Drug Enforcement, and Highway Safety;

(b) The BIA, Office of Justice Services, NPA oversees the BIA Indian Highway Safety Program which seeks "to reduce the number and severity of traffic crashes in Indian Country by supporting education, and enforcement, as well as Safe Tribal Communities Programs;

(c) The Highway Safety Act of 1966, U.S.C. Title 23, Section 402, and the U.S. Department of Transportation is providing funding to assist Indian tribes in implementing traffic safety projects;

(d) The Pueblo and BIA Office of Justice Services, NPA have recognized the need to implement a Child Passenger Safety Seat Program solely for the benefit of the Pueblo de San Ildefonso;

(e) The Pueblo de San Ildefonso recognizes the need to implement a Child Passenger Safety Seat Program in order to reduce the high number of crashes and their resulting fatalities, and safety seat injuries within the exterior boundaries of the Pueblo de San Ildefonso.

14.1.2.020

The Pueblo de San Ildefonso Tribal Council hereby approves the Pueblo de San Ildefonso the Pueblo de San Ildefonso FY 2016 BIA Indian Highway Child Passenger Safety Seat Program and future years.

Part 14.1.3 Senior Center Act and the Evaluation of Services and Funding Administered by the Eight Northern Indian Pueblos Council, Inc.

14.1.3.010 Short Title

This enactment shall be known as the "SENIOR CENTER ACT".

(May 3, 2021, SI-R21-006.)

14.1.3.020 Purpose

The purpose of this act is to authorize and direct the completion of a comprehensive study, evaluation, and analysis of the current funding and management of the Senior Center ("Senior Center") including options for improving the delivery of services.

(May 3, 2021, SI-R21-006.)

14.1.3.030 Findings

The Council hereby finds and declares that:

(a) The Pueblo currently contract with the Eight Northern Indian Pueblos Council, Inc. ("ENIPC") to operate the Senior Center and to provide services to seniors; and

(b) ENIPC receives certain federal and state funds on behalf of the Pueblo to pay for the operation and management of the Senior Center, including hiring and directing the Site Manager for the Senior Center; and

(c) The Governor and Council Representatives have received requests from members of the Pueblo to evaluate the quality and delivery of services at the Senior Center provided by ENIPC, including the improvement of services and whether the Pueblo should continue to contract with ENIPC to operate the Senior Center; and

(d) The Council desires to study and evaluate the current funding and management of the Senior Center prior to any decision to modify the current arrangement with ENIPC.

(May 3, 2021, SI-R21-006.)

14.1.3.040 Senior Center Report of 2021

(a) The Council hereby authorizes and directs the Governor and the Director of Health and Human Services, Elderly Services Committee, or a designee, to conduct a written, comprehensive review, evaluation, and analysis of the current programs and services provided at the Senior Center by the ENIPC and related issues ("Senior Center Report of 2021").

(b) The Senior Center Report shall include the following:

(1) An evaluation of the operation of the Senior Center by ENIPC including the current programs and services;

(2) An evaluation of the management and staffing of the Senior Center by ENIPC including the selection and supervision of the Site Manager by ENIPC;

(3) The types and amounts of funding received by ENIPC on behalf of the Pueblo to operate Senior Center;

(4) The administrative fees charged by ENIPC to operate the Senior Center;

(5) The process to modify or cancel the current arrangement with ENIPC as the contractor that operated the Senior Center including whether incremental steps could be taken to move towards a Senior Center that is operated, managed, and administered by the Pueblo;

(6) An evaluation of the funding requirements to operate the Senior Center and the funding necessary to provide improved and/or expanded services;

(7) Any other observations and recommendations to assist the Council in evaluating delivery of services by ENIPC at the Senior Center including methods to improve the delivery, type, and scope of services.

(c) The Senior Center Report of 2021 shall be completed and delivered to the Council within sixty (60) days of the effective date of the Senior Center Act.

(May 3, 2021, SI-R21-006.)

Part 14.1.4 The Community Health Assessment and Community Health Improvement Plan Authorization Act

14.1.4.010 Short Title

This enactment shall be known as the “THE COMMUNITY HEALTH ASSESSMENT AND COMMUNITY HEALTH IMPROVEMENT PLAN AUTHORIZATION ACT” (“Act”).

(March 28, 2022, SI-R22-001.)

14.1.4.020 Purpose

The purpose of this Act is to authorize the Pueblo de San Ildefonso’s (Pueblo) request to partner with the Albuquerque Area Southwest Tribal Epidemiology Center (AASTEC) to complete a Community Health Assessment and Community Health Improvement Plan (CHA).

(March 28, 2022, SI-R22-001.)

14.1.4.030 Findings

The Council hereby finds and declares that:

(a) The Pueblo has received funding from the AASTEC to allow the Pueblo’s Tribal Health Council and Department of Health and Human Services to participate and complete a CHA; and

(b) The Pueblo’s Tribal Health Council and Department of Health and Human Services operates under the authority provided by the Council and acts on behalf of the Pueblo for health matters; and

(c) The Pueblo has primary goals and objectives of improving and maintaining the health and wellness needs of its people, including activities to address the burden of chronic diseases (i.e., cardiovascular disease, stroke, and type 2 diabetes), COVID-19, behavioral health and substance use/misuse, and underlying risk factors (i.e., tobacco use, obesity, physical inactivity, poor nutrition, generational and environmental trauma, and adverse childhood experiences (ACEs); and

(d) The Pueblo acknowledges the last Community Health Assessment (CHA) was completed in 2015 and the needs of the community have changed; and

(e) The Pueblo de San Ildefonso acknowledges the benefits that can be achieved from completing a CHA, in the area of public health and community healthcare; and

(f) Health status measures are often not available at the community level; and

(g) The CHA was designed to provide a useful and useable tool for tribes to measure their overall health status; and

(h) The AASTEC will work closely with the Pueblo to identify appropriate health indicators and to ensure that the project is conducted in a confidential and culturally sensitive manner; and

(i) The identified health indicators may be measured using community surveys, interviews, existing records, or other sources; and

(j) It is necessary to approve the indicators required in order to complete the CHA; and

(k) The Council has reviewed this project and has determined it is in the Pueblo’s best interest to move forward with the CHA in collaboration with the AASTEC.

(March 28, 2022, SI-R22-001.)

14.1.4.040 Authorizations

(a) The Council hereby authorizes the Pueblo’s Tribal Health Council and Department of Health and Human Services to participate and complete a CHA, beginning March 14, 2022 and ending September 29, 2022.

(b) The authority granted by the Resolution shall remain in effect until withdrawn by the Council or until the term of the new contract has expired, and the Governor is the authorized agent for the Tribe and shall represent the Council in any negotiations, changes and/or approval of the contract agreement.

(March 28, 2022, SI-R22-001.)
